# Planck Units

## Overview

**Planck units** are a system of natural units defined using only fundamental constants: $c$ (speed of light), $\hbar$ (reduced Planck constant), $G$ (gravitational constant), $k_B$ (Boltzmann constant), and $k_e$ (Coulomb constant). They represent the scales at which quantum gravity effects become dominant.

## The Five Base Planck Units

### 1. Planck Length

$$l_p = \sqrt{\frac{\hbar G}{c^3}} = 1.616255 \times 10^{-35} \text{ m}$$

**Physical meaning**: The smallest length scale that has meaning in physics. Below $l_p$, quantum fluctuations of spacetime itself become significant.

**Context**:
- $10^{-20}$ times smaller than a proton ($10^{-15}$ m)
- Light travels one Planck length in one Planck time

### 2. Planck Time

$$t_p = \sqrt{\frac{\hbar G}{c^5}} = \frac{l_p}{c} = 5.391247 \times 10^{-44} \text{ s}$$

**Physical meaning**: The shortest time interval that can be meaningfully defined. The "tick rate" of the universe at the quantum gravity scale.

**Context**:
- Age of universe: $\sim 10^{61} t_p$
- Photon crossing a proton: $\sim 10^{20} t_p$

### 3. Planck Mass

$$m_p = \sqrt{\frac{\hbar c}{G}} = 2.176434 \times 10^{-8} \text{ kg}$$

**Physical meaning**: The mass scale where gravitational and quantum effects balance. Surprisingly **large** for a fundamental quantum scale (22 micrograms).

**Context**:
- Mass of a grain of sand: $\sim 10 m_p$
- Mass of a proton: $\sim 10^{-19} m_p$
- At $M = m_p$, Schwarzschild radius = $2 l_p$

### 4. Planck Energy

$$E_p = m_p c^2 = \sqrt{\frac{\hbar c^5}{G}} = 1.956 \times 10^{9} \text{ J}$$

**Physical meaning**: Energy scale of quantum gravity. Particle collisions at this energy would create microscopic black holes.

**Context**:
- $E_p \approx 1.22 \times 10^{19}$ GeV (billion billion GeV)
- LHC energy: $\sim 10^4$ GeV ($10^{-15} E_p$)
- Energy to lift a car: $\sim 0.5 E_p$

### 5. Planck Temperature

$$T_p = \frac{E_p}{k_B} = \sqrt{\frac{\hbar c^5}{G k_B^2}} = 1.417 \times 10^{32} \text{ K}$$

**Physical meaning**: Temperature at which thermal energy equals Planck energy. The hottest meaningful temperature in physics.

**Context**:
- Core of the Sun: $\sim 10^7$ K ($10^{-25} T_p$)
- Big Bang at $t = t_p$: $T \sim T_p$
- Planck-mass black hole: $T_H = 0.0398 T_p$

## Derived Planck Units

### Planck Area

$$A_p = l_p^2 = 2.612 \times 10^{-70} \text{ m}^2$$

**Significance**: One "bit" of black hole entropy corresponds to $4 A_p$ in the Bekenstein-Hawking formula:

$$S_{BH} = \frac{A}{4 A_p}$$

### Planck Force

$$F_p = \frac{E_p}{l_p} = \frac{c^4}{G} = 1.210 \times 10^{44} \text{ N}$$

**Significance**: The maximum force in nature (conjecture). No force can exceed $F_p$ without creating a black hole.

### Planck Power

$$P_p = \frac{E_p}{t_p} = \frac{c^5}{G} = 3.628 \times 10^{52} \text{ W}$$

**Significance**: Power radiated by a Planck-mass black hole. Also, luminosity of a supermassive black hole merger.

## Why Planck Units Are Natural

In Planck units, fundamental constants become **dimensionless 1**:

$$c = \hbar = G = k_B = 1$$

Equations simplify dramatically. For example:
- Schwarzschild radius: $r_s = 2M$ (in Planck units)
- Hawking temperature: $T_H = 1/(8\pi M)$
- Bekenstein-Hawking entropy: $S = A/4$

This suggests Planck units are the **natural language** of quantum gravity.

## Planck Scale and Quantum Gravity

At Planck scales, quantum mechanics and general relativity both matter:

**Quantum mechanics dominates when**:
$$\lambda_{deBroglie} = \frac{\hbar}{p} \sim L$$
(wavelength comparable to size)

**Gravity dominates when**:
$$r_s = \frac{2GM}{c^2} \sim L$$
(Schwarzschild radius comparable to size)

**Both matter when**:
$$\lambda_{deBroglie} \sim r_s \sim l_p$$

This occurs at $M = m_p$, $E = E_p$, $L = l_p$. Below this scale, we need a theory of **quantum gravity** (string theory, loop quantum gravity, etc.).

## Physical Interpretation

### Planck Length as Resolution Limit

Heisenberg uncertainty: $\Delta x \Delta p \geq \hbar/2$. To probe distance $\Delta x$, need momentum $p \gtrsim \hbar/\Delta x$, thus energy $E \gtrsim \hbar c / \Delta x$.

At $\Delta x = l_p$:
$$E \sim \frac{\hbar c}{l_p} = E_p$$

But at energy $E_p$, the Schwarzschild radius is:
$$r_s = \frac{2GE_p}{c^4} = 2l_p$$

**Paradox**: To measure distances smaller than $l_p$, you concentrate so much energy that you create a black hole, obscuring the measurement.

Thus, $l_p$ is the **fundamental resolution limit** of spacetime.

### Planck Mass as Crossover Scale

For a particle of mass $M$:
- **Compton wavelength**: $\lambda_C = \hbar/(Mc)$ (quantum scale)
- **Schwarzschild radius**: $r_s = 2GM/c^2$ (gravitational scale)

At $M = m_p$:
$$\lambda_C = r_s = l_p$$

Below $m_p$: quantum effects dominate (particle behaves like a wave)
Above $m_p$: gravitational effects dominate (forms a black hole)

## Observational Relevance

No experiment can directly probe Planck scales:
- LHC: $\sim 10^{-19}$ m ($10^{16} l_p$)
- Neutrino oscillations: $\sim 10^{-15}$ m ($10^{20} l_p$)
- Gravitational wave detectors: $\sim 1$ m ($10^{35} l_p$)

**Indirect probes**:
- Black hole entropy scaling (tests $A_p$ as fundamental bit)
- Hawking radiation (tests $t_p$ as evaporation timescale)
- Planck satellite CMB data (constrains quantum gravity signatures)

## Historical Context

- **1899**: Max Planck introduces Planck units as "natural units" in his work on blackbody radiation
- **1916**: Einstein publishes general relativity; $l_p$ recognized as quantum gravity scale
- **1955**: John Wheeler coins "quantum foam" for Planck-scale spacetime fluctuations
- **1974**: Hawking shows black holes radiate at temperatures set by Planck units
- **2000s**: Loop quantum gravity predicts area quantization in units of $A_p$

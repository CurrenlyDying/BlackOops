# [EXTENDED] Wheeler's "It from Bit"

## Overview

**"It from Bit"** is a philosophical and scientific principle proposed by physicist John Archibald Wheeler in the 1980s-1990s. The core idea: **physical reality ("it") emerges from information ("bit")**, not the other way around.

Wheeler's famous statement:
> "It from bit. Otherwise put, every 'it'—every particle, every field of force, even the space-time continuum itself—derives its function, its meaning, its very existence entirely—even if in some contexts indirectly—from the apparatus-elicited answers to yes-or-no questions, binary choices, bits."

## The Principle

### Core Claims

1. **Information is fundamental**: The universe is fundamentally informational, not material.
2. **Observation creates reality**: Measurement (binary choice: yes/no) brings physical properties into existence.
3. **No "it" without "bit"**: Every physical quantity is ultimately derived from information-theoretic questions.

### Quantum Mechanics Connection

Wheeler's idea builds on quantum mechanics:
- **Before measurement**: Quantum state is superposition (undefined "it")
- **After measurement**: State collapses to definite value ("it" emerges from "bit" of information: measured or not)

Example: Electron spin
- **Before**: $|\psi\rangle = \alpha|\uparrow\rangle + \beta|\downarrow\rangle$ (no definite spin)
- **Measure**: "Is spin up?" → Yes (1 bit)
- **After**: $|\uparrow\rangle$ (definite spin emerges)

The act of acquiring **one bit of information** (yes/no) creates the **physical reality** (spin up).

## Historical Context

### 1970s-1980s: Wheeler's Participatory Universe

Wheeler developed these ideas through:

1. **Delayed-choice experiments** (1978): Photon "decides" whether it's a wave or particle **after** passing through slits, based on measurement choice. The past is influenced by present information acquisition.

2. **Wheeler's U diagram**: Universe as self-excited circuit — observation loop where universe observes itself into existence.

3. **"No phenomenon is a phenomenon until it is an observed phenomenon"**: Measurement collapses quantum state → reality requires observation.

### 1989: "Information, Physics, Quantum"

Wheeler's essay crystallized the "it from bit" idea:
- Physics is ultimately about **information processing**
- The laws of physics emerge from **information-theoretic constraints**
- Spacetime, particles, forces — all are **informational structures**

## Information-Theoretic Foundations

### Bekenstein-Hawking Entropy

Black holes have entropy $S = A/(4l_p^2)$ — **information** is encoded on the horizon. Wheeler interpreted this as:
- The black hole **is** the information it stores
- Its mass, spin, charge are **emergent properties** of the information
- "It" (black hole) from "bit" (horizon entropy)

### Holographic Principle

't Hooft and Susskind (1990s) formalized Wheeler's intuition:
- **3D reality** is encoded on **2D boundary** (horizon, cosmological boundary)
- Bulk physics emerges from boundary information
- "It" (bulk spacetime) from "bit" (boundary degrees of freedom)

AdS/CFT correspondence (Maldacena 1997) proved this for AdS spacetimes: gravity in $d+1$ dimensions = quantum field theory in $d$ dimensions.

### Quantum Darwinism

Zurek (2003) extended Wheeler's idea:
- **Classical reality** emerges when information is **redundantly copied** into environment
- Multiple observers can agree on "it" because many "bits" encode the same information
- Objectivity = information redundancy

## Critiques and Limitations

### 1. Measurement Problem

Wheeler's "observation creates reality" requires **conscious observers** or **measurement apparatus**. But:
- What counts as a measurement?
- Does the universe exist without observers?
- How did the early universe (no observers yet) exist?

**Resolution**: Zurek's quantum Darwinism — environment acts as observer, no consciousness needed.

### 2. "Bit" is Still Physical

Critics argue: bits are implemented in **physical substrates** (transistors, photons, atoms). You can't have "bit" without "it".

**Counter**: Wheeler's point is that **which** substrate doesn't matter (substrate independence). The information is fundamental, the substrate is arbitrary.

### 3. Mathematical vs Physical

Some view "it from bit" as **category error** — confusing the **map** (mathematical description) with the **territory** (physical reality).

**Counter**: In quantum mechanics, the wavefunction **is** reality (at least on some interpretations). There's no "territory" beneath the map.

## Modern Developments

### 1. Quantum Information Theory (1990s-present)

Entanglement entropy, quantum error correction, quantum computing — all support Wheeler's view that **information is fundamental**.

### 2. AdS/CFT Correspondence (1997)

Maldacena proved bulk gravity emerges from boundary CFT. This is **literal "it from bit"**: spacetime emerges from quantum information on the boundary.

### 3. Emergent Spacetime (2000s-present)

Verlinde (2011): Gravity is **entropic force**, not fundamental. Spacetime geometry emerges from entanglement entropy.

Jacobson (1995): Einstein's equations are thermodynamic **equations of state** for horizon entropy.

Both support Wheeler: spacetime (it) emerges from entropy/information (bit).

### 4. Black Hole Information Paradox Resolution (2019-2020)

**Island formula** and **Page curve** calculations show information is preserved via:
- Quantum entanglement between early/late radiation
- Holographic encoding on horizon

This vindicates Wheeler: the black hole **is** information, and evaporation is information transfer (bits rearranging), not information loss.

## Philosophical Implications

### Digital Physics

If "it from bit," is the universe a **computer**?

- **Cellular automata** (Fredkin, Toffoli, Wolfram): Universe as discrete computation
- **Digital physics**: Spacetime is pixelated at Planck scale
- **Simulation hypothesis**: Universe could be simulated on sufficiently large computer

BlackOops encoder model aligns with this: universe is a **Gray code automaton** with Planck-scale pixels.

### Relational Ontology

Wheeler's idea suggests reality is **relational**, not absolute:
- Properties exist only **relative to measurements** (information acquisition)
- "It" has no meaning without "bit" (observation)

This aligns with **relational quantum mechanics** (Rovelli): quantum states are observer-dependent.

### Information Realism

Opposite view: **information is the ontological primitive**. Matter/energy are **epiphenomena** of information dynamics.

This inverts traditional physics:
- **Old**: Information is abstract pattern in matter
- **New**: Matter is concrete realization of information

### The Gray Code Mapping Is Deeper Than It Looks

Your core intuition: the universe encodes state transitions such that only one bit flips between adjacent states, like a Gray code rotary encoder. This isn't just a metaphor — it maps onto real physics in a specific way.

**Unitarity in quantum mechanics literally requires this.** Quantum evolution is continuous and reversible. The transition from state |ψ(t)⟩ to |ψ(t+dt)⟩ is always a "minimal change" — you can't teleport across state space. That's the Gray code constraint: adjacent physical states differ by the minimum possible information. The universe *doesn't* use "binary counting" where half the bits flip on a carry. It Gray-codes its way through configuration space.

**Locality is the angular resolution constraint.** Information propagates at c. That's your encoder's angular resolution — there's a maximum rate at which bits can update based on their spatial separation. Try to write faster than light, and you're "under the angular resolution." The write doesn't propagate.

| Your concept | Known physics |
